French Toast

Description

French toast, known as "pain perdu" in French, translates to "lost bread," referring to its use of stale or leftover bread. This classic dish has a rich history and is enjoyed in various forms around the world.

French toast typically consists of bread slices soaked in a mixture of beaten eggs, milk, and often flavored with spices such as cinnamon and vanilla. The soaked bread is then fried or baked until golden brown and served hot, often topped with powdered sugar, maple syrup, fruit preserves, or fresh fruit.

The texture of French toast can vary depending on personal preference and regional variations. Some prefer it soft and custardy on the inside, while others like it crispy on the outside. The choice of bread also plays a role in the final texture and flavor, with options ranging from thick slices of brioche or challah to hearty whole grain bread.

History

The origins of French toast can be traced back to ancient civilizations where cooks utilized leftover bread to avoid waste. The dish has been referenced in various cultures throughout history, each with its own variation.

One of the earliest recorded versions of French toast dates back to the Roman Empire, where it was known as "Pan Dulcis." Bread soaked in milk and then fried was a popular dish among the Romans.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 tablespoons corn syrup
  • 6 (1-inch thick) slices French bread
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1 ½ cups half-and-half cream
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 1 teaspoon brandy-based orange liqueur (such as Grand Marnier)
  • ¼ teaspoon salt

Steps

  1. Prepare Baking Dish: Melt butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Add brown sugar and corn syrup; stir until sugar is dissolved. Pour into a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Prepare Bread: Remove crusts from bread and arrange in the baking dish in a single layer. Whisk eggs, half and half, vanilla, brandy, and salt together in a bowl. Pour over the bread. Cover and chill at least 8 hours, or overnight.
  3. Preheat: Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Meanwhile, remove baking dish from the refrigerator and bring to room temperature.
  4. Bake: Bake, uncovered, in the preheated oven until puffed and lightly browned, 35 to 40 minutes.
