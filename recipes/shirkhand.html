Shirkhand

Description

Shrikhand is a traditional Indian dessert hailing from the western state of Maharashtra, although it is enjoyed across various regions of India.

It is a creamy, sweet dish made from strained yogurt, flavored with sugar, cardamom, and sometimes saffron. Shrikhand has a rich, silky texture and a delicate balance of sweetness and aromatic spices.

History

According to food historian K. T. Achaya, shrikhand was first made around 500 BC. His book Indian Food: A Historical Companion states, "To dewater curd, it was hung in a muslin bag for a few hours; sugar and spices added to the mass yielded shikharini (identical with modern day shrikhand), first noted around 500 BC.”. This procedure is still followed today.

The 11th century Kannada poet Chavundaraya II gave a recipe for shikharini as strained yogurt mixed with flavorings and spices in his book on agriculture, the Lokopakara." The Soopa Shastra, a cookbook written in 1508 by the Jain king Mangarasa III, also mentions shrikhand.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups plain yogurt (preferably full-fat)
  • 1/2 cup powdered sugar (adjust to taste)
  • 1/4 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • A pinch of saffron strands (optional)
  • 1-2 tablespoons warm milk (if using saffron)
  • Chopped nuts (such as almonds, pistachios) for garnish

Steps

  1. Strain the Yogurt: Place a muslin cloth or a clean kitchen towel over a strainer. Pour the yogurt onto the cloth and gather the edges to form a bundle. Hang the bundle over the sink or a bowl and let it drain for about 4-6 hours, or until the excess liquid (whey) has been drained out and you're left with thick, creamy yogurt.
  2. Prepare Saffron (Optional): If using saffron, crush the saffron strands slightly and soak them in 1-2 tablespoons of warm milk. Let it sit for 10-15 minutes to infuse the milk with saffron flavor and color.
  3. Mix Yogurt and Sugar: Once the yogurt is strained, transfer it to a mixing bowl. Add powdered sugar and cardamom powder. Mix well until the sugar is completely dissolved and the mixture is smooth.
  4. Add Saffron (Optional): If using saffron, pour the saffron-infused milk into the yogurt mixture and stir until well combined. This will give your Shrikhand a beautiful golden color and a subtle saffron flavor.
  5. Chill: Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a lid and refrigerate the Shrikhand for at least 1-2 hours to allow the flavors to meld and the dessert to chill.
  6. Serve: Once chilled, transfer the Shrikhand to serving bowls or glasses. Garnish with chopped nuts like almonds and pistachios. You can also sprinkle a few saffron strands on top for decoration, if desired.
